Frequently Asked Questions

Find instant answers to common questions about traveling to Switzerland.

Switzerland is part of the Schengen Area. US, Canadian, Australian, and many other passport holders can visit visa-free for up to 90 days within a 180-day period. However, requirements vary by nationality, so we recommend checking with the Swiss embassy or consulate in your country. We're happy to provide personalized guidance based on your citizenship.

Switzerland is beautiful year-round, each season offering unique experiences:

  • • Winter (Dec-Mar): Perfect for skiing, snowboarding, and winter wonderland experiences
  • • Spring (Apr-May): Wildflower blooms, fewer crowds, and pleasant hiking weather
  • • Summer (Jun-Aug): Ideal for mountain hiking, lake activities, and outdoor festivals
  • • Autumn (Sep-Nov): Stunning fall colors, harvest season, and comfortable temperatures

Switzerland has four official languages: German (spoken by about 63%), French (23%), Italian (8%), and Romansh (0.5%). The good news? English is widely spoken in tourist areas, hotels, and restaurants. Most Swiss people are multilingual, and you'll find that communication is rarely a barrier. We provide helpful language basics in our travel guides to enhance your cultural experience.

Switzerland uses the Swiss Franc (CHF), not the Euro. While some tourist areas accept Euros, you'll get better value using Swiss Francs. Credit cards are widely accepted, and ATMs are readily available. We recommend withdrawing cash from ATMs for the best exchange rates, and always having some cash for smaller establishments, mountain huts, and public transportation.

Switzerland has one of the world's best public transportation systems. The Swiss Travel Pass offers unlimited travel on trains, buses, and boats, plus free admission to many museums. Trains are punctual, clean, and offer spectacular scenic routes. We highly recommend experiencing at least one scenic train journey like the Glacier Express or Bernina Express. Our journey planning tools help you navigate the system with ease.

Switzerland is known for high quality and premium experiences, which comes with higher costs compared to many destinations. However, there are ways to manage your budget: stay in mountain huts or hostels, cook some meals, use the Swiss Travel Pass for transportation savings, and visit free attractions like hiking trails and lakeside promenades. We can help you plan a trip that matches your budget while still experiencing Switzerland's magic.
